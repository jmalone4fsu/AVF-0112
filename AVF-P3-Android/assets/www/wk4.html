Question 1: What are the 'top selling' app categories in the U.S.?

The most popular categories are games, news, maps, social networking and music.

Question 2: What are the 'top selling' app categories in Japan?

The most popular categories are social networking (texting), AR (Augmented Reality), e-commerce, and games

Question 3: What are the 'top selling' app categories in Great Britain?

The most popular categories in Great Britain are entertainment, music, and games

Question 4: Based on your three answers above, what might you infer about each of the markets listed?

US/UK markets are geared towards games and entertainment. Japan market trends towards productive apps for networking and e-commerce.

Question 5: What percentage of Android users have the latest/greatest OS in the U.S? in Japan? in Great Britain? How might these differences impact development?

From all the reports I've researched, less than 1% of worldwide Android phones are on the latest/greatest OS. What's more interesting is the fact that 3/4 of the Android phones in the global market will NEVER see an update to the latest/greatest OS.

Question 6: What is the average cost and bandwidth limits per user (in general) in each of the three markets? How may this impact development?

Just recently in the US, most mobile providers have dropped the unlimited plans or throttled them. The UK, average cost is $44/month for unlimited bandwidth (some throttling occurs). In Japan, most carriers provide unlimited bandwidth at the four times faster speeds than the US.

It's important to remember data charges and speeds when developing an app so that you don't abuse these limits for your users.

Question 7: And, finally, what would be your suggested strategy in project topic and global market choice? Feel free to have a first and second choice.

US/UK - I would suggest gaming or social network projects for the US/UK markets. When marketing in Japan, AR and social networking. Any project that is bandwidth intensive I would push towards the Japan markets where speeds are blazing fast and bandwidth is unlimited.
